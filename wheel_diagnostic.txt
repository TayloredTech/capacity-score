THE WHEEL, MADE PERSONAL
Which Way Is Your Wheel Turning?
A four minute self read for senior leaders. Not a quiz, a mirror. Answer for the month you are actually in, not the one you intend to have. Nobody is marking this but you.
How it works.  Sixteen statements, in four parts, one for each point of the wheel. For each, mark how often it is true in a typical month: Rarely, Sometimes, Often, or Almost always. The numbers above each column are the points. Add them up at the end. The statements describe the wheel turning the wrong way, so a higher number means more drag, not more failing.

16 to 31.  Turning with you.  The wheel is carrying you more than it is costing you. Hold it. The work now is to protect the few things that are keeping it spinning the right way, because the wrong direction arrives quietly and by default, usually right when the load spikes.
32 to 47.  Starting to slip.  The wheel is stalling and beginning to turn back on you. This is the most useful place to be reading this, because it is early. You can feel the drag but you are not yet underneath it. One deliberate change now is worth ten later.
48 to 64.  Turning against you.  The wheel is spinning the wrong way and your own drive is feeding it, because the most driven people spin it fastest. This is not a discipline problem and it will not yield to gripping harder. It is a design problem, and that is good news, because design is fixable.
Where to push first
You cannot turn this wheel from the top. You cannot order your state to improve or force good leadership out of an empty tank. But two points are always within reach, and your scores show you which one is dragging hardest.
Compare Part 2 and Part 3.  Whichever scored higher is where your wheel is being pulled the wrong way, and it is also the one you can actually reach. Start there. Not both. One.
If Part 2 is higher, push behaviour.  Put one keystone back, at the same time every day, defended like a meeting you cannot move. The walk, the sleep window, the movement. Not all of them. One, this week.
If Part 3 is higher, push environment.  Engineer one protected pocket and guard it. The early hour, the commute, the door closed, the notifications off. Built on purpose, not hoped for at the end of the day.
What this cannot tell you
You can now feel which way your wheel is turning. What this cannot tell you is why it is turning that way.
The direction, you can feel. The engine underneath it, how fast you deplete, how slowly you recover, how hard the same pressure lands on you compared to the person beside you, that you cannot read by feel. It runs below the level of willpower, which is exactly why willpower never reaches it.
That is the next layer down, and it is the one that turns a felt direction into a designed one. If your wheel is turning against you, and you have already tried gritting harder and it did not hold, you are precisely who the work below this is built for.
Not more disciplined. Better designed.
This is a mirror to think with, not a diagnosis.
