Why an audit

You can’t manage what you’ve stopped noticing.

Low energy stops feeling low once you’ve lived in it long enough. Most leaders don’t realise how far their baseline has drifted until they see it measured.

You’ve probably got fragments already. A wearable score here, an annual blood panel there, maybe an AI summary of both. What’s missing is the connected picture: what your biology is predisposed to, what it’s doing right now under real load, and what to change first. That’s what the audit builds.

What we measure

Five instruments. One connected picture.

Deliberately lean. Each instrument earns its place by answering part of the sustain-the-role question.

DNA panel

Your fixed predispositions. How you metabolise, recover and respond to stress at the genetic level, so everything else is read against your baseline, not a population average.

Biological age

The headline number. How fast your body is actually aging against your calendar age, and the single most honest measure of accumulated load.

Nutrient status bloods

Iron, B12, vitamin D, magnesium and the other levers behind energy and focus. This is usually where the quickest wins hide.

Wearable baseline

An Oura ring or Whoop band, yours to keep. Live sleep, recovery and strain data from week one, while the lab work processes.

14-day glucose

A continuous glucose monitor over two working weeks. It shows exactly when and why your afternoons fall over, meeting by meeting, meal by meal.

The shape of it

Ten weeks, most of them yours.

The audit is designed around a full calendar. Your hands-on time is a kickoff, two working weeks of wearing a sensor, and one readout.

Weeks 1–8

Weeks 9–10

The readout

A private session with both founders. You leave knowing your three quickest wins and precisely why they’re yours.

What you keep

The Executive Biological Blueprint.

A written document, built by both founders from your data alone. Current-state architecture, in plain language, with a short list you can act on the same week:

  • Your three quickest wins, ranked by effect against effort
  • The lowest-barrier changes for the load you actually carry
  • The one area with the biggest payoff, if you only fix one thing
  • Your wearable and glucose baselines, so future change is measurable
  • ExecOS access, so the plan turns into a daily directive rather than a PDF in a drawer

Where it leads

The audit finds the leaks. The advisory fixes them.

For most clients the audit is the whole engagement. Clear picture, three wins, a system to run them in.

Some readouts surface things worth going deeper on: cortisol rhythm, gut health, cognitive load, the full diagnostic battery. That work lives in the Private Advisory, a twelve-month engagement limited to five seats a year. Audit clients see the invitation at the readout. There’s no obligation either way, and we only raise it when the data says there’s something worth pursuing.
