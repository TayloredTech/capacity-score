The Biology of Leadership

How to read your wheel.

A short field guide to the Capacity Mirror. Not a test, not a diagnosis. A mirror to think with, so you can see which way your capacity is turning and which point to reach for first. It is an early draft of an idea I am still shaping through the book, a working hypothesis rather than a finished answer.

The four points

Your wheel has four points

Capacity turns on four points, and Biology underpins all of them. You do not change your biology by force. You change it through the two points that are always within reach: Behaviour and Environment. Shift those, and your biology, and the spin, shift with them. The four are interrelated, so pull on one and the others move with it.

BiologyThe foundation the whole wheel runs on. Your physical engine: sleep, recovery, energy, the body you are actually operating from. You reach it through the two levers below, not head-on.
BehaviourA lever within reach. Your daily choices and habits: how you sleep and move, whether you show up for your future self, and the small decisions that compound over time.
EnvironmentThe other lever within reach. The conditions around you, your load and demands, and the people you spend your time with.
LeadershipHow the spin shows up. How you direct, and the state you set for others. The top of the wheel, which is exactly why you cannot force it directly.
Spin is the thing

The wheel is always turning

Left to itself, the wheel turns counter-clockwise, and counter-clockwise depletes you. That is the default. Building capacity means actively spinning it the other way, clockwise. Every point either helps spin it clockwise (a lift) or lets it slip counter-clockwise (a drag). The Mirror reads how often each point is doing which, in a typical month.

A high drag reading is not a failing. It is a signal. It tells you which point is asking for attention first.
The four states

What the whole wheel is doing

Put the four points together and the wheel reads as one of four states. None is a verdict. It is the month you are actually in.

Peak
The wheel is turning cleanly with you. Capacity is high, and the hard, high-value work you push into it compounds. Aim your biggest efforts here.
Steady
Turning the right way, with a little resistance. Solid ground. Not a day to force, not a day to coast.
Conserve
The wheel is stalling and starting to turn back on you. You can feel the drag but you are not under it yet. One deliberate change now is worth ten later.
Restore
The wheel has turned against you and you are underneath it. The work now is recovery, not push. Protect the base and let it come back.
How to use it

You cannot turn the wheel from the top

You cannot order your state to improve, or force good leadership out of an empty tank. But two points are always within reach: Behaviour and Environment. Change those, and your biology, the foundation, changes with them. The Mirror shows you which one is dragging hardest, so you can put one keystone back, at the same time every day, defended like a meeting you cannot move.

Not more disciplined. Better designed.

If you want to know why

The Mirror shows you which way your wheel is turning. What it cannot tell you is why, or which actions will lift your wheel hardest. The most powerful lever is not the same for everyone. It runs all the way down to your individual biology, your genetics. Generic advice gives generic results. The Capacity Audit maps your own biology in detail, so you can act on what actually moves you.
